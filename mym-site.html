Case Study

MYM – Website Redesign

2025 Role: Lead UX / UI Branding Web Design
Websites UX Branding

Overview

Marvin Yoder Ministries needed a refreshed brand identity and website that better served their audience—Christians in the Word of Faith circle—many of whom are older and less familiar with technology. I led the UX research, branding, and UI design, ensuring the site was accessible, visually aligned with the new brand, and easy to navigate.

Problem → Solution → Impact

  • Problem: The old website lacked brand cohesion, clear navigation, and accessibility for older users.
  • Solution: A complete brand refresh with new color palette, typography, and logo, paired with a layer-cake pattern layout to simplify scanning and navigation.
  • Impact: Improved communication with followers, streamlined donation process, and a site that visually reflects the ministry’s mission and identity.

Pain points (from user research)

  • Audience needs quick, easy access to the author’s materials.
  • Predominantly elderly users less comfortable with technology.
  • Website colors made navigation difficult for some users.
  • Feedback called for clearer, more intuitive navigation.

Highlights

  • Layer-cake pattern layout for scannability.
  • Brand color palette in deep blue and gold for clarity and recognition.
  • Logo redesign to modernize and strengthen brand presence.
  • Improved content hierarchy and better calls to action.
  • Donation flow simplified for a non-profit audience.

My role

  • Lead UX Designer & Researcher
  • Brand identity development
  • Wireframing & prototyping
  • UI design & usability testing

Audience

Christians in the Word of Faith community, many of whom are elderly and less tech-savvy.

Goals

Create a brand-aligned, easy-to-use website that gives followers access to materials, itinerary, and resources while improving donations and communication.

Constraints & notes

Coordinating with a traveling client limited real-time feedback; branding and usability adjustments were guided heavily by remote feedback and user testing.

Iteration after Usability Study 1

Usability testing revealed issues with navigation clarity and contrast. The updated design uses more defined brand colors, improved hierarchy, and simplified pathways to content and donations.

Screen gallery

What I learned

Brand identity and accessibility go hand-in-hand—color choices, contrast, and layout patterns directly impact usability, especially for older audiences. Iteration based on real feedback made the navigation clearer and the site more engaging for its community.
