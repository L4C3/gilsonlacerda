Case Study

MYM App – Mobile UX

2025 Role: Lead UX / UI Research Prototyping
Mobile UX UI Design

Overview

MYM (Marvin Yoder Ministries) is a mobile experience that brings the author’s sermons, books, and event info to people who don’t regularly use computers. I led UX research and design, produced paper wireframes and high‑fidelity mocks, and iterated after a usability study to improve navigation, clarity, and brand expression.

Problem → Solution → Impact

  • Problem: The public wants easy access to the author’s material, but many in the audience are older and less comfortable with technology.
  • Solution: A simple, guided mobile app with clear entry points—Messages, Store, and Calendar—plus sign‑in, product detail, and checkout flows.
  • Impact: Better findability of content, fewer steps to purchase, and a friendlier first‑time experience for non‑technical users. The first usability pass led to a redesigned home with stronger hierarchy and brand color cues.

Pain points (from user research)

  • People want a fast, straightforward way to reach the author’s materials (messages and books) without hunting.
  • A significant portion of the audience is elderly and not very tech‑savvy, so labels, contrast, and navigation must be extremely clear.
  • Early layout didn’t communicate priorities; the redesign focuses the eye on search, recommended content, and the primary tabs.
  • Store and checkout needed to feel predictable and safe for first‑time mobile buyers.

Highlights

  • Home with search and “Popular Books,” plus quick links to Events and Store.
  • Book Store grid → Product detail → Add to bag → Checkout.
  • Bottom navigation refined after usability study to improve wayfinding.
  • Consistent brand color and improved contrast for older users.
  • Paper wireframes → digital mocks → iteration after study 1.

Client context: Mr. Marvin Yoder is a Bible instructor, pastor, author, and itinerant minister known within Word of Faith circles. The app expands access beyond the website for audiences who primarily use phones.

My role

  • Lead UX designer & researcher
  • Information architecture & navigation
  • UI design & prototyping
  • Usability planning and synthesis
  • Brand/application of visual system

Audience

Christians within the Word of Faith community; many older, low‑tech users.

Goals

Make the author’s content and books easily accessible on mobile for users who may not use computers.

Constraints & notes

Launched new website during a major conference; post‑launch feedback and 1:1 conversations validated the need for a mobile app and guided iterations.

Screen gallery

What I learned

Usability sessions quickly revealed where hierarchy and labels weren’t doing enough work—especially for older users. I learned to let go of early ideas and lean into clearer navigation, higher contrast, and a home screen that surfaces the most wanted actions first.
