Case Study

Rhema Bible Church – Website Redesign

2024–2025 Role: Web Design & Development WordPress / Elementor Architecture‑inspired UI
Design System UX Web Design

Overview

The redesign of Rhema Bible Church’s website was guided by the architectural principles of Andrea Palladio—symmetry, proportion, and classical order—to digitally echo the harmony and welcoming grandeur of the church’s main auditorium. The outcome is a calm, structured, and reverent experience that helps people find their next step—whether that’s attending a service, watching online, exploring ministries, or getting involved.

Problem → Solution → Impact

  • Problem: The legacy experience scattered key actions (visit, watch, give, get involved) and made discovery across ministries feel fragmented.
  • Solution: A Palladian‑inspired layout system and clear information architecture that elevates primary paths—Start Here, Ministries, Engage/Serve, Give, and Watch—with strong cross‑links to related content.
  • Impact: A timeless visual language that mirrors the auditorium’s balance and dignity, faster wayfinding for new and returning visitors, and improved clarity around services, media, and next steps.

Highlights

  • Palladio principles translated into grid, spacing, and typographic hierarchy.
  • Primary entry points: Start Here, Ministries, Engage/Serve, Give, Watch Live/On‑Demand.
  • “Cathedral‑calm” UI: restrained palette, generous white space, and clear CTAs.
  • Componentized Elementor sections for schedules, media, bulletin, leadership, and app promos.
  • Responsive, accessible patterns with semantic landmarks and WCAG‑aware contrast.

Design Philosophy

Andrea Palladio’s influence—recognized by the U.S. Congress as the “Father of American Architecture”—informed the site’s structure and tone. We translated symmetry and proportion into a disciplined grid, balanced white space, and typographic pairings that feel classic yet readable. The goal was to evoke the same sense of balance, beauty, and reverence people feel when entering the auditorium—now from the homepage.

  • Symmetry & Proportion: A 12‑column grid and modular spacing scale for rhythmic flow.
  • Classical Hierarchy: Framed hero regions and pediment‑like heading stacks create a welcoming “entry.”
  • Purposeful Contrast: Brand accents reserved for decisive actions—Start Here, Watch, Give, Serve.

Information Architecture & UX

The homepage leads with immediate orientation and next steps. Key sections—Start Here, Ministries, Engage (Serve, LINK Teams, community care), Give, Watch Live/On Demand, Podcasts, Leadership, Bulletin, and App—are presented as clear, scannable entry points with contextual links between them. This allows first‑time visitors and long‑time members to land, decide, and act quickly.

  • Primary paths: Visit services, watch online, explore ministries, get involved, give.
  • Cross‑service discovery: Inline prompts connect related content (e.g., ministries → serve opportunities).
  • Mobile‑first patterns: Sticky CTAs, scannable cards, collapsible FAQs reduce cognitive load.

Accessibility & Performance

  • WCAG‑aware color contrast and semantic landmark regions.
  • SVG logos and modern image formats with lazy‑loading.
  • Motion‑reduction preferences respected for decorative effects.
  • Reusable blocks keep DOM lean and improve maintainability.

Key Sections

  • Welcome & Services: Prominent service times and a friendly invitation to attend.
  • Watch Live & On Demand: Direct access to live streams and recent services.
  • Ministries: Kids, Youth, Young Adults, Español, School of the Bible, and more.
  • Engage: Serve, We‑Care Center, LINK Teams, community initiatives.
  • Podcasts & Media: 24/7 listening options and media resources.
  • Leadership: Meet the pastors and leadership team.
  • Bulletin & App: Weekly bulletin and mobile app callouts to stay connected.

Each area uses consistent heading scales, card systems, and CTA bars, so users always know what to do next.

Content Strategy

  • Value‑led headlines and short leads.
  • Benefit bullets replace generic descriptions.
  • CTAs that set clear expectations (“Watch Live,” “Start Here,” “Give,” “Serve”).

Implementation

  • Platform: WordPress + Elementor Pro with a reusable section library.
  • Components: Heroes, section headers, schedules, media grids, leadership cards, bulletin/app promos.
  • Media: SVG marks and optimized images; lazy‑loading across galleries and section imagery.
  • Governance: Editor‑friendly blocks for quick updates by non‑technical staff.

Outcomes

A site that embodies the serenity and order of Rhema’s physical space while making digital next steps unmistakably clear. Visitors can watch, give, explore ministries, serve, and connect in fewer clicks—with a design that will age gracefully.

  • Architecture‑inspired visual system that reinforces the church’s identity.
  • Clear primary actions and faster orientation for new visitors.
  • Reusable components and editor‑friendly blocks for ongoing updates.

What I Learned

  • Translating physical architecture into digital rhythm yields a calmer UX.
  • Strong IA and consistent components reduce maintenance overhead.
  • Small copy refinements (headlines, leads, CTA labels) dramatically aid decision‑making.
