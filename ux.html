Case Study

Taniti Island – UX Website

2025 Role: UX Designer (solo) Adobe XD Wireframes → Prototype Usability Testing
UX Web Prototype

Overview

A tourism website concept for Taniti Island designed to guide families from “Where do we start?” to confident trip planning. The project followed a four‑week UX process—from research and personas to wireframes, high‑fidelity prototyping in Adobe XD, and usability testing—focused on clear information architecture and meaningful calls‑to‑action.

Problem → Solution → Impact

  • Problem: First‑time visitors don’t know where to click first and feel overwhelmed by transportation choices.
  • Solution: Establish a clear homepage hierarchy with a primary CTA (“Plan Your Trip”) and reorganize transport content into a single, well‑labeled section with supporting context and a map.
  • Impact: Improved findability and decision‑making: users can quickly start planning, explore attractions, and choose transport and lodging without confusion.

Timeline (4 weeks)

  • Week 1 – Research & Planning: scope, competitive analysis, personas, site map.
  • Week 2 – Wireframing & Content: low/mid‑fi wireframes in Adobe XD; first‑pass content; peer review.
  • Week 3 – Prototyping: high‑fi prototype with basic animations; internal usability tests; refinements.
  • Week 4 – Testing & Finalization: broader usability testing; improvements; responsive optimizations; export & submit.

Stack

Adobe XD, Information Architecture, Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability Testing

User Persona: Charles Carter

Charles (35) is a marketing professional and father of three seeking a family‑friendly vacation that balances beaches, rainforest hikes, and cultural sightseeing. He travels by air, plans to rent a car, stays 6 days in a hotel, and wants clear paths to attractions, lodging, and transportation.

  • Party: Family of 5 • Budget: Middle‑income • Goal: Family vacation
  • Needs: Simple navigation, standout CTA, transparent transport options
  • Interests: Local attractions, adventure activities, cultural experiences

Primary Tasks (for testing)

  1. Choose a hotel based on preferences
  2. Explore local attractions
  3. Navigate transportation options
  4. Choose a restaurant by dietary preferences
  5. Locate emergency services

Homepage – Applied Changes

  • Added a clear hero CTA (“Plan Your Trip / Explore Taniti”).
  • Turned generic buttons into prominent card links for primary sections.
  • Removed confusing personalized tabs.
  • Planned a hero video to set context and engagement.

Transport Page – Applied Changes

  • Clarified hero copy to explain options at a glance.
  • Consolidated all transport options into a single, well‑organized section.
  • Added a map to highlight public transportation and wayfinding.
